# Channel Guidance

Use this file when deciding channel mix, message length, or sequencing.

## Email

Best for:

- richer storytelling
- detailed offer explanation
- multiple products or content blocks
- imagery and modular content

Watch-outs:

- weaker urgency than mobile channels
- heavier production dependencies
- inbox competition

## SMS

Best for:

- urgent reminders
- short offers
- strong single CTA
- last-chance or deadline-driven sends

Watch-outs:

- limited space
- high sensitivity to frequency
- stricter consent and quiet-hour expectations

## WhatsApp

Best for:

- conversational or service-led promotional outreach
- reminders with richer context than SMS
- high-trust re-engagement where the brand has an established relationship

Watch-outs:

- template approval or platform policy constraints
- market-specific usage norms
- risk of feeling intrusive if the value exchange is weak

## Push

Best for:

- immediate return-to-app prompts
- behavior-based nudges
- short reminders

Watch-outs:

- low room for nuance
- depends on app install and notification permissions

## In-App or Inbox

Best for:

- reinforcing the same offer after click or app open
- follow-up steps after an outbound touch
- contextual education at point of action

Watch-outs:

- not suitable as the only channel when reach is the primary need

## Sequencing Heuristics

- Start with email when education, explanation, or visual merchandising matters most.
- Start with SMS or push when urgency is the core lever and the ask is simple.
- Use WhatsApp when a more conversational, trust-led follow-up is appropriate and consent supports it.
- Use secondary channels to reinforce the primary message, not to repeat it word for word.
- Tighten message length and sharpen the CTA as urgency increases across the sequence.
